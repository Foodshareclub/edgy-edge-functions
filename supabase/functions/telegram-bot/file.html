There are several ways in which food waste contributes to climate change 1. Agricultural production: Growing, harvesting, and transporting food requires energy and other resources. When food is wasted, all of these resources are also wasted, contributing to greenhouse gas emissions. 2. Landfills: When food waste ends up in landfills, it decomposes and releases methane, a potent greenhouse gas. Methane is approximately 28 times more effective at trapping heat in the atmosphere than carbon dioxide over a 100-year time frame. 3. Energy use: Food waste can also contribute to climate change through the energy used to produce it. For example, if food is grown using fossil fuels, such as in the case of some industrial agriculture practices, this can contribute to greenhouse gas emissions. 4. Water use: Producing food requires water, and when food is wasted, this water is also wasted. In some areas, the production of food is a significant contributor to water scarcity, which can have environmental and social impacts. It is difficult to quantify exactly how much of climate change is caused by food waste, as there are many other factors at play. However, reducing food waste is an important strategy for mitigating the impact of climate change, as it can help to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, conserve resources, and protect the environment. iberdrola
